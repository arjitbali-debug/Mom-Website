Biodynamic Craniosacral Therapy

A gentle, non-forceful approach that uses light touch, therapeutic presence and careful attention to the body as a whole.

What is it?

Biodynamic Craniosacral Therapy (BCST) is a complementary hands-on practice. Sessions are generally quiet and use light contact while the practitioner attends to subtle patterns of motion, stillness, tension and physiological organization.

The biodynamic model emphasizes the person's inherent resources and the creation of conditions in which settling, integration and self-regulation may occur. It does not diagnose disease and is not a substitute for medical, psychological or emergency care.

Autonomic regulation

Polyvagal Theory as a related framework

Stephen Porges

Polyvagal Theory, developed by neuroscientist Stephen Porges, is a theoretical framework concerning autonomic state, safety, social engagement and defensive responses. Many somatic practitioners use its language to discuss regulation and co-regulation.

Learn about Polyvagal Theory from the Polyvagal Institute →

An important distinction

Polyvagal Theory does not constitute proof that craniosacral therapy treats a particular medical condition. The two are presented as related conceptual frameworks—not as the same method and not as evidence of a guaranteed clinical outcome.

Health disclaimer: Evidence for craniosacral therapy varies by condition and remains limited for many proposed uses. Results cannot be guaranteed. Seek qualified medical care for diagnosis, new or worsening symptoms, emergencies, pregnancy concerns, newborn health issues or significant pain.
